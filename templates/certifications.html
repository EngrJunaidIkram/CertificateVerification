ISO certifications have become a baseline expectation in many industries. They signal to customers, regulators, and partners that your organization operates with defined processes, manages risk properly, and takes quality seriously.

At ISO Global Standards, we work with businesses across sectors to achieve the certifications that matter most to their operations. Below is a breakdown of the ten standards we see the highest demand for heading into 2025.

01

ISO 9001: Quality Management System (QMS)

ISO 9001:2015 remains the most widely adopted management system standard in the world. It provides a framework for organizations to ensure their products and services consistently meet customer expectations and applicable regulatory requirements.

Why it matters:

  • Drives operational efficiency across departments
  • Increases customer satisfaction through consistent delivery
  • Often required for tenders and international contracts
02

ISO 14001: Environmental Management System (EMS)

ISO 14001 gives organizations a structured way to manage their environmental impact. It covers everything from waste reduction to resource efficiency and regulatory compliance, and is increasingly expected by clients and investors alike.

Key benefits:

  • Measurable reduction in environmental footprint
  • Easier compliance with environmental regulations
  • Lower waste and more efficient use of resources
03

ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety (OHSMS)

Workplace safety is a legal and moral responsibility. ISO 45001 provides the framework for identifying hazards, assessing risks, and implementing controls that protect employees and reduce incidents.

Key advantages:

  • Fewer workplace incidents and injuries
  • Stronger employee confidence and morale
  • Demonstrated compliance with health and safety law
04

ISO 27001: Information Security Management System (ISMS)

With data breaches becoming more frequent and costly, ISO 27001 offers a proven methodology for managing information security risks. It applies to any organization that stores, processes, or transmits sensitive information.

Commonly adopted by:

  • Technology and software companies
  • Financial services and banking
  • Any business handling personal or confidential data
05

ISO 20000-1: IT Service Management (ITSM)

ISO 20000-1 sets out requirements for an IT service management system. It helps organizations align their IT services with business objectives and deliver them reliably and efficiently.

Well suited for:

  • Managed IT service providers
  • Cloud and hosting companies
  • Organizations with complex IT infrastructure
06

ISO 22000: Food Safety Management System (FSMS)

ISO 22000 applies to every organization in the food chain. It combines the principles of HACCP with a management system approach to ensure that food products are safe at every stage, from production through to the consumer.

Core outcomes:

  • Consistent food safety across the supply chain
  • Greater consumer and retailer confidence
  • Compliance with national and international food safety regulations
07

ISO 50001: Energy Management System (EnMS)

Energy costs are a significant expense for most businesses. ISO 50001 provides a systematic approach to monitoring, measuring, and improving energy performance over time.

Particularly relevant for:

  • Manufacturing and production facilities
  • Large commercial operations
  • Organizations with sustainability commitments
08

ISO 22301: Business Continuity Management (BCM)

Unexpected events can halt operations at any time. ISO 22301 helps organizations prepare for, respond to, and recover from disruptions so that critical functions continue with minimal interruption.

Benefits:

  • Structured risk identification and mitigation
  • Faster recovery when incidents occur
  • Greater confidence from clients and insurers
09

ISO 29993:2017: Non Formal Education and Training Services

This standard applies to organizations that deliver learning services outside formal education systems, such as corporate training providers, vocational centres, and professional development institutes.

Why it matters:

  • Consistent quality in training delivery
  • International recognition for non formal education providers
  • A framework for measuring and improving learner outcomes
10

ISO 21001: Management System for Educational Organizations (EOMS)

Built specifically for schools, universities, and training centres, ISO 21001 focuses on inclusive education, learner satisfaction, and measurable outcomes. It gives educational institutions a clear management framework to work within.

What it delivers:

  • Better structured learning environments
  • Stronger accountability for educators and administrators
  • Global credibility for the institution
